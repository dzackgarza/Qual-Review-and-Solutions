The Symmetric Group

  • A cycle is even ⇔ product of an even number of transpositions.

  • A cycle of even length is odd

  • A cycle of odd length is even

Mnemonic: the parity of a $k\dash$cycle is the parity of k − 1.

The alternating group is the subgroup of even permutations, i.e. [ A_n ] where $\sign(\sigma) = (-1)^{m}$ and m is the number of cycles of even length.

Every σ ∈ An has an even number of odd cycles (i.e. an even number of even-length cycles).

Counting Theorems

[H G H G.]

The order of every element divides the size of G, i.e.
$$ g\in G \implies o(g) \divides o(G) \implies g^{\abs G} = e .$$

There does not necessarily exist H ≤ G with $\abs H = n$ for every $n \divides \abs{G}$. Counterexample: $\abs{A_4} = 12$ but has no subgroup of order 6.

For every prime p dividing $\abs{G}$. there is an element (and thus a subgroup) of order p.

This is a partial converse to Lagrange’s theorem, and strengthened by Sylow’s theorem.

Group Actions

An action of G on X is a group morphism [ :G X &X \ (g,x) &gx ] or equivalently [ : G &(X) \ g (x _g (x) gx) ] satisfying

  1. e ⋅ x = x
  2. g ⋅ (h ⋅ x) = (gh) ⋅ x

Useful fact: $\ker \psi = \intersect_{x\in X} G_x$ is the intersection of all stabilizers.

A group action $G\actson X$ is transitive iff for all x, y ∈ X there exists a g ∈ G such that g ⋅ x = x. Equivalently, the action has a single orbit.

Reminder of notation: for a group G acting on a set X,

  • $G\cdot x = \theset{g\cdot x \suchthat g\in G} \subseteq X$ is the orbit

  • $G_x = \theset{g\in G \suchthat g\cdot x = x} \subseteq G$ is the stabilizer

  • X/G ⊂ 2X is the set of orbits

  • $X^g = \theset{x\in X \suchthat g\cdot x = x} \subseteq X$ are the fixed points

Note that being in the same orbit is an equivalence relation which partitions X, and G acts transitively if restricted to any single orbit.


$$ \abs{G\cdot x} = [G: G_x] = \abs{G} / \abs{G_x}\quad \text{if $G$ is finite} $$

Mnemonic: G/Gx ≅ G ⋅ x.

Examples of Orbit-Stabilizer

  1. Let G act on itself by left translation, where g ↦ (h ↦ gh).

  • The orbit G ⋅ x = G is the entire group

  • The stabilizer Gx is only the identity.

  • The fixed points Xg are only the identity.

  1. Let G act on itself by conjugation.

  • G ⋅ x is the conjugacy class of x (so not generally transitive)

  • $G_x = Z(x) \definedas C_G(x) = \theset{g \suchthat [g, x] = e}$, the centralizer of x.

  • Gg (the fixed points) is the center Z(G).

The number of conjugates of an element (i.e. the size of its conjugacy class) is the index of its centralizer, [G : CG(x)].

  1. For a fixed proper subgroup H < G, let G act on its cosets $G/H = \theset{gH\suchthat g\in G}$ by left translation.

  • G ⋅ gH = G/H, i.e. this is a transitive action.

  • $G_{gH} = gHg\inv$ is a conjugate subgroup of H

  • (G/H)G = ∅

If G is simple, H < G proper, and [G : H] = n, then there exists an injective map $\phi: G \injects S_n$.

This action induces ϕ; it is nontrivial since gH = H for all g implies H = G; $\ker \phi \normal G$ and G simple implies ker ϕ = 1.
